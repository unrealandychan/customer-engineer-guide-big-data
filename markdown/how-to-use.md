## Daily Habits (15–20 min, every day until interviews)

These compound fast with minimal daily time:

1. **Read one section of a question bank** — not to memorize, to get comfortable with question variety
2. **Answer one question out loud** — no notes, timer on. This is the only practice that counts.
3. **Read the "Answer Skeleton" / "Full Pattern Summary"** from yesterday's topic — 5 minutes for retention

## The Most Common Mistakes to Avoid

### In Your Prep
- **Passive reading** — reading a question bank is not practice. Answer questions out loud. Always.
- **Skipping story personalization** — template prompts in `star-stories.md` mean nothing until your real experience is there.
- **Over-preparing answers to known questions** — interviewers will probe off-script. Practice the framework, not the script.
- **Ignoring the Lessons line** — Google interviewers specifically score for this. Practice ending every behavioral answer with what you'd do differently.

### In the Actual Interview
- **Starting to answer before clarifying** — always ask at least one question first, even if you know the answer.
- **Saying "we" for your actions** — "I" for what you did; "we" only for the team context.
- **Staying silent while thinking** — narrate your thinking: "Let me work through this..."
- **Giving a result without a number** — estimate if you don't have exact figures; state your assumption.
- **Skipping trade-offs** — every architectural recommendation has a trade-off. Name it.
