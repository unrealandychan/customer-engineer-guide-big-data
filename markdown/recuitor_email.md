Interview B: General Cognitive Ability (GCA)
GCA, otherwise known as problem solving, is one of the key focuses for us for this interview process at Google. It is also one of the more difficult areas to prepare for. When we ask these questions, we want you to really understand the problem, prepare your thought process (thinking out loud is fine!), find great solutions, make logical choices, and explain your ideas clearly.
The interviewer will be assessing the following areas:
General Cognitive Ability - understanding the problem, prepares, identifying solutions, support solutions and communicating clearly
We ask open-ended questions to see how you approach and solve a problem, either something you have done in your past experience, or maybe a hypothetical question that you have not yet experienced.  For work-related questions, be prepared to talk about complex challenges you faced.  What happened? What did you do? How did you solve it?  For hypothetical questions, you might get something that you haven't quite thought about yet, and that's the purpose. We want to see how you approach a problem, and how you go about solving it.

It is important to remember that there is no single right answer, your ability to explain your thought process and how you use data to inform decisions is the most important part. A clear explanation and supporting evidence (we love data and numbers!) is a good way to form your answer.
This interview will test your logical thinking and problem solving ability through hypothetical questions (hypothetical situations that maybe you haven't experienced yet, pls clarify ambiguity in the question to collect more info, apply logical thinking and give a structured answer), as well as behavioral questions (past experiences and the way you approached certain real-life situations, so pls prepare some past examples using the STAR approach: Situation, Your Task, Actioned you took, Result).

Interview C: Culture Fit - Googleyness \& Leadership (G\&L) 
Leadership means the person can dive in, work well with others and get things done with minimal hand-holding. Leadership for us is defined as stepping up in your job to get things done and to take ownership of a project or a process. 
The Googleyness attribute measures how well the candidates can contribute to the Google experience and thrive in fast-paced, diverse environments with small, collaborative teams. Googleyness can sometimes feel like a vague concept, but we like to test certain behavior and biases to action that you have to ensure you are the right culture fit for being successful with us.  Some of those areas that we look at include your ability to work in a fast-paced, changing environment and how you handle ambiguity.

We might ask you about ethical decisions or situations you have faced, your ability to value different perspectives different from your own, and your ability to challenge the status quo when necessary. This might be around a decision that you didn't agree with, or when you received resistance from someone on your team — how did you manage that? We are also likely to ask you questions that revolve around putting the user first, or a situation about the way you care about your team and focus on doing the right thing.
In summary, make sure you prepare yourself by considering the following information:

- Examples of you making a complex business decision which different viewpoints were leveraged.
- Examples of you are under big pressure when in a Deal/Projects, but you make innovative ways to accomplish goals/complete projects instead of break rules/compromise your value.
