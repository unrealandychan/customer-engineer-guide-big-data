# Interview C — G&L: Question Bank

20 real questions from Google interview reports. Organized by category.
Andy (Platform CE Team Manager) will run this — he manages CEs, so every question has a CE-role lens.

**Use SPSIL structure (Situation → Problem → Solution → Impact → Lessons) for all behavioral answers.**

---

## Category 1: Googleyness Questions

### Comfort with Ambiguity + Bias for Action
1. **"Tell me about a time you had to handle trade-offs and ambiguity simultaneously."**
   → Use Story 5 (diverse viewpoints) or Story 1A (leading without authority). Name the ambiguity explicitly. Show you made a decision anyway.

2. **"Tell me about a time you challenged the status quo — and what happened."**
   → Use Story 2 (pushback) or Story 4 (humility/changed mind). Name the conventional thinking you rejected and the rational justification for the challenge.

3. **"Tell me about a time when you had to come up with a creative solution to solve a problem."**
   → This is a free-form story — use any story where your solution was non-obvious. Name the conventional approaches you rejected and why.

4. **"Tell me about a time you received difficult feedback and how you responded."**
   → Use Story 4 (humility). Show you separated your ego from the feedback. Quantify what changed as a result.

### Doing the Right Thing
5. **"Tell me about your biggest failure — what happened?"**
   → This is a "do the right thing + humility" question in disguise. Own the failure clearly, show no deflection, and make the Lessons line the centerpiece.

6. **"Tell me about a time you had an ethical dilemma or were pressured to cut corners."**
   → Use Story 3 (ethical pressure). Be explicit about the value you protected and what you were willing to give up.

7. **"Describe a situation where you did the right thing when it was hard or costly."**
   → Same as above. Name the cost you accepted and why it was worth it.

### Ownership + High Standards
8. **"Tell me about a time you created something from nothing."**
   → Build something new — product, process, framework, platform. Show you initiated without being asked.

9. **"Why Google? Why this role?"**
   → Don't fake this. Be specific: reference the CE specialist role's mandate (specilaist CE, deep technical, prototype/POC builder), Google Cloud's position in data and AI, and something genuine about building at Google scale.
   → Suggested frame: "What draws me is the specialist CE model — having the depth to solve problems that generalist teams can't, and being the connective tissue between product engineering and enterprise customers."

10. **"What makes a great Customer Engineer? What makes a bad one?"**
    → Great: technical depth + customer empathy + ability to translate between worlds + bias for building concrete solutions. Bad: order-taker with no initiative, or deep technical but can't make it land with a customer.

### Thinking Freely
11. **"If you had coffee with Sundar Pichai, what would you talk about?"**
    → Pick something genuine — not flattery. A technology trend, an observation about the CE role, a market dynamic you think Google is positioned to win. Shows you think at scale and are intellectually curious.

12. **"Tell me about your favorite Google product and why."**
    → Pick something related to your role: BigQuery, Vertex AI, Looker, or Gemini. Explain what you love about the design decision — not just the feature set. Show genuine product appreciation.

## Category 2: Leadership Questions

### Leading Without Authority
13. **"Tell me about a time you demonstrated leadership even though you weren't the formal manager."**
    → Use Story 1A or 1B. Show: you identified the gap, stepped in, brought others along, and delivered.

14. **"Tell me about a time you led a team through a difficult situation."**
    → Combine Story 1A (technical leadership) with crisis management elements from your STAR Story 3 (production incident). Show: clarity under pressure, communication, follow-through.

### Project Ownership + Delivery
15. **"Tell me about a time you were the end-to-end owner of a project."**
    → Show the full arc: problem definition → design → build → ship → measure → learn. No hand-offs without accountability.

16. **"Tell me about a time you improved a process at a previous workplace."**
    → Pick something operational — data pipeline, deployment process, customer onboarding, incident management. Quantify before and after.

### Team + People Leadership
17. **"How would you ensure your team is diverse and inclusive?"**
    → Use Story 5 (diverse viewpoints) as the foundation. Show you actively seek perspectives different from your own. Name specific practices: inclusive decision-making sessions, rotating who presents, ensuring junior voices are heard.

18. **"How would you address a skill gap or personality conflict on a team?"**
    → Triangle Method: (1) diagnose root cause, (2) address it directly with the individual privately before escalating, (3) structure a systemic fix (training plan, pairing, role clarity).
    → Show "no-ego leadership": you don't need the problem to make the other person look bad — you need the team to win.

19. **"Tell me about a time you developed or retained a team member."**
    → Show genuine investment in someone else's growth. Not just "I gave feedback" — show curiosity about their goals, specific coaching, and a measurable outcome for them.

### Self-Development
20. **"Where do you see your career moving? What would you love to learn at Google?"**
    → Be specific: "I want to deepen in [AI/ML infrastructure for enterprise data / multi-cloud architecture / data governance at scale]. At Google scale, I'd face problems I've never encountered and work alongside the engineers who built these products."
    → Show that learning is intrinsic — not just credential-chasing.
