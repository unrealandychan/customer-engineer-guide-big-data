# Mock Interview Script — All 3 Rounds
**Use this to simulate the real thing. Have a friend/partner read the interviewer lines, or read them yourself and pause the timer.**

---

## How to Use This

**Rules:**
- No looking at your notes during the mock. If you blank, keep going — that's data.
- Time every answer with a stopwatch. Behavioral: target 3.5–4 min. Hypothetical: target 4–5 min.
- After each answer, note: Did you use a number? Did you say "I" not "we"? Did you close with a Lessons line or metric?
- Do the full mock dry run 1–2 days before each interview, not the morning of.

## MOCK INTERVIEW B — GCA (Gene, 45 min)

### Opening (Gene)
> "Hi Eddie — thanks for jumping on. I'm Gene, I manage the Data Analytics Field Sales team. Today is the GCA round — I'll be asking you some behavioral and problem-solving questions. No single right answer — I just want to understand how you think. Ready?"

*[You: confirm ready. Set your stopwatch.]*

---

### Question 1 — Behavioral (Gene)
> "Tell me about a time you made a decision based on data when the data was incomplete or ambiguous."

*[You respond: STAR + Lessons. Target: 3.5–4 minutes.]*

**After your answer — Gene's follow-up probes:**
> 1. "What proxy data did you use? Why that data specifically?"
> 2. "How did you communicate the uncertainty to your stakeholders?"
> 3. "What would have happened if that key assumption had been wrong by 30%?"
> 4. "What would you do differently if you were starting that project today?"

*[Answer each follow-up in 45–60 seconds.]*

---

### Question 2 — Hypothetical (Gene)
> "Let's shift to a scenario. How would you design an analytics platform for a retail customer that is merging three different brands — each with their own cloud and on-prem data infrastructure? The CIO needs a one-year plan for AI activation and data monetization."

*[You respond: Clarify → Decompose → Deep Dive → Conclude + Metric. Target: 4–5 minutes.]*

**Required opener — you MUST ask this clarifying question first:**
> [You: "Before I dive in — what's the CIO's primary constraint: timeline, budget, or risk tolerance? And are all three brands on different cloud providers, or is there any overlap?"]

**After your answer — Gene's follow-up probes:**
> 1. "Why BigQuery over Snowflake for this? The customer has existing Snowflake contracts."
> 2. "How do you handle the identity resolution problem if customers exist in only one brand and have never shared their contact info?"
> 3. "Walk me through what 'AI activation in 12 months' actually means architecturally — what specific AI use cases can you deliver?"
> 4. "How would you measure success at the 6-month checkpoint for the CIO's board presentation?"

*[Answer each in 60–90 seconds.]*

---

### Question 3 — Behavioral (Gene, if time permits)
> "Tell me about a time you had to convince an engineer or technical stakeholder to adopt an approach they were resistant to."

*[You respond: STAR + Lessons. Target: 3.5 minutes.]*

**Follow-up:**
> 1. "What made them resistant initially?"
> 2. "What data or argument actually changed their mind?"

## MOCK INTERVIEW C — G&L (Andy, 45 min)

### Opening (Andy)
> "Hi Eddie — I'm Andy. I run the Platform CE team here at Google. Today I'll be asking about your leadership experiences and what we call Googleyness. These are behavioral questions about real things you've done — not hypotheticals. I'll probably ask a lot of follow-ups. Ready?"

*[You: confirm ready. Set your stopwatch.]*

---

### Question 1 — Googleyness / Ambiguity (Andy)
> "Tell me about a time you had to handle trade-offs and ambiguity simultaneously."

*[You respond: SPSIL. Target: 3.5–4 min.]*

**Required in your answer:**
- Name the ambiguity explicitly — not "things were unclear" but "specifically, [X] was undefined while simultaneously [Y constraint] existed"
- Show a decision you made DESPITE the ambiguity, not after it resolved
- "Values Protected" line: "What I was protecting here was..."
- Lessons line: specific and forward-looking

**Andy's follow-up probes:**
> 1. "How did others on the team feel about your approach?"
> 2. "What if your chosen path had been wrong — what was your fallback?"
> 3. "Looking back, what ambiguity did you tolerate that you should have resolved earlier?"
> 4. "What's your general approach to operating in ambiguous environments — how do you personally manage it?"

*[Each probe: 45–60 seconds.]*

---

### Question 2 — Leadership Without Authority (Andy)
> "Tell me about a time you demonstrated leadership even though you weren't the formal manager."

*[You respond: SPSIL. Target: 3.5–4 min.]*

**Required in your answer:**
- Make it CLEAR you had no formal authority — say it explicitly
- Name the gap: "No one was stepping up because [reason]"
- Show HOW you brought others along — not just that you acted
- Include the follow-through: what happened after your initiative

**Andy's follow-up probes:**
> 1. "How did the people you were leading react to someone without authority stepping up?"
> 2. "Was there anyone who pushed back? How did you handle that?"
> 3. "What would have happened if you hadn't stepped in?"
> 4. "How do you know when to step up vs. when to wait for someone else to take the lead?"

---

### Question 3 — Doing the Right Thing (Andy)
> "Describe a situation where you did the right thing when it was hard or costly."

*[You respond: SPSIL. Target: 3.5–4 min.]*

**Required in your answer:**
- Name the cost you accepted — not a free ethical choice
- Name the value you protected: "I did this because [user trust / data integrity / team integrity]"
- Be honest if the outcome was imperfect

**Andy's follow-up probes:**
> 1. "Was this the right hill to die on? What was the actual risk if you'd gone along?"
> 2. "How did you protect the relationship with the person who was applying the pressure?"
> 3. "How is that relationship today?"
> 4. "What would you do differently in how you handled it — not the decision, but the execution?"

---

### Question 4 — CE Role Assessment (Andy)
> "What makes a great Customer Engineer? And on the flip side — what makes a bad one?"

*[You respond: direct answer. Target: 90 seconds.]*

**Then Andy asks:**
> "Where on that spectrum do you land? What's your own development area?"

*[You respond: honest self-assessment. Target: 45 seconds.]*

---

### Question 5 — Career / Fit (Andy, closing)
> "Where do you see your career going? And — specifically — what would you want to learn or develop at Google?"

*[You respond. Target: 90–120 seconds.]*

**Required:**
- Specific GCP/data area you want to go deeper in (AI infrastructure? Multi-cloud governance? Real-time analytics?)
- Specific reason Google is the right place to learn that (products at the frontier + engineers who built them + scale of problems)
- Genuine, not performance — if it's not real to you, it will show

---

### Mock Debrief — Interview C
- [ ] Did you name ambiguity/constraints explicitly, not just "things were unclear"?
- [ ] Did every story have a named "Values Protected" line?
- [ ] Did you say "I" not "we" for your actions?
- [ ] Did you name the cost or consequence in the ethical story?
- [ ] Did your "why Google" answer include something specific and genuine?
- [ ] Did follow-up probes expose any story where you couldn't answer without notes?

If any probe left you blank — that story needs one more rehearsal pass before the real interview.

## MOCK INTERVIEW D — PRESENTATION (William, 45 min)

### Opening (William)
> "Hi Eddie — I'm William, the hiring manager. Today is your presentation round. I'll play two roles: I'll be acting as the technical domain expert for part of the Q&A, and then I'll shift to a VP of Strategy lens. You have 30 minutes for the presentation and then we'll do Q&A. Go ahead whenever you're ready."

*[You: say your opening lines from delivery-guide.md. Start timer.]*

---

### Presentation (30 min)

Run your full presentation against the `delivery-guide.md` timing script.

**Check at each section:**
- 2:00 — End of Title & Context. Did you state the one-line problem?
- 6:00 — End of Specialist Challenge. Did you name all 4 reasons off-the-shelf fails?
- 18:00 — End of Technical Deep-Dive. Did all 3 demos run? Did you explain identity resolution, windowing/watermark, and BigQuery ML?
- 23:00 — End of Trade-offs. Did you defend BigQuery and name when you'd choose differently?
- 27:00 — End of Business Value. Did you state all 4 ROI claims with the assumption?
- 30:00 — Did you close with the 3 CTA bullets?

---

### Q&A — Technical Stakeholder (William in Gene mode)

> Q1: "I'm skeptical of Dataflow over Databricks. We have a lot of PySpark on Azure today with Brand C. Why wouldn't I just use Databricks for the processing layer?"

> Q2: "Explain to me exactly how your identity resolution handles a customer who has accounts at Brand A and Brand C but used a different email for each brand."

> Q3: "The demand forecast model — walk me through how you'd validate it's actually accurate before putting it in production."

> Q4: "What happens if Brand B's Oracle database isn't ready for Datastream in Phase 1? What's your fallback?"

> Q5: "How do you handle schema evolution? Brand A tells you they're adding 40 new columns to their orders table — what breaks?"

---

### Q&A — VP of Strategy (William switches register)

> Q6: "This sounds expensive. What's the budget, and what's the ROI case I can take to the board?"

> Q7: "We're worried about vendor lock-in — if we build everything in BigQuery and Google's products, what's our exit strategy in 3 years?"

> Q8: "The board has asked us about data monetization — can you make that more concrete? What does it look like in practice for us in year 1?"

> Q9: "My concern is that we'll start this project, spend 6 months, and not see any business value for another 6 after that. How do you address the 'valley of death' between starting and delivering?"

> Q10: "What's the one thing most likely to cause this project to fail? And what's your mitigation?"

---

### Mock Debrief — Interview D
- [ ] Did you hit all 6 time checkpoints within ±1 minute?
- [ ] Did all 3 demos work and stay under their time budget?
- [ ] Did you deliver the 4 ROI numbers with assumptions stated?
- [ ] In Q&A, did you handle the Databricks objection without dismissing it?
- [ ] Did you switch registers naturally between technical and business Q&A?
- [ ] Did you handle the vendor lock-in question with a concrete exit strategy?
- [ ] Did you stay composed when the "expensive" question came up?
- [ ] Last words of the presentation — did you end with the 3 CTA bullets?
