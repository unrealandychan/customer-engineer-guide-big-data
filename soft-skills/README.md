## The Mental Model: You Are a Doctor, Not a Salesperson

> The best consultants think like doctors:
> 1. **Diagnose before prescribing** — ask questions before pitching solutions
> 2. **Earn trust through expertise** — give away insight freely; the deal follows
> 3. **Bad news early** — if GCP isn't the right fit for a workload, say so
> 4. **Long-term health > short-term win** — recommend what's right, not what's easiest to sell

A doctor who prescribes medicine before examining the patient loses trust immediately.  
A consultant who pitches before understanding the problem loses credibility just as fast.

## The 5 Core Soft Skills for a Google Pre-Sales Engineer

### 1. Active Listening
> Hearing not just what is said, but *why* it's being said, and *what isn't* being said.

### 2. Structured Communication
> Answering questions in a clear structure — bottom-line up front, then evidence, not rambling.

### 3. Pushback Navigation
> Staying calm, validating concerns, then redirecting — never getting defensive.

### 4. Whiteboard Facilitation
> Driving a technical conversation in a room without losing the audience or the thread.

### 5. Persona Adaptability
> Adjusting depth, vocabulary, and pace depending on whether you're talking to a CTO, a DBA, or a CFO.

## How to Practice

**Solo practice (minimum viable):**
1. Read a scenario
2. Say your response out loud (not in your head — speaking is different from reading)
3. Time yourself: aim for 60–90 seconds per response
4. Record yourself on your phone; play it back

**Partner practice (recommended):**
1. Give your partner the "Customer" script
2. You play the consultant
3. Partner gives you pushback from the scenarios
4. Debrief: did you validate first? Did you stay calm? Did you pivot to business value?

**The 30-second rule:**  
If you cannot explain your response in 30 seconds, you don't understand it well enough yet.
